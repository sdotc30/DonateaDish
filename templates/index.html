Current Scenario

On average, an Indian household wastes about 50 kg of food per person annually, amounting to millions of tonnes nationwide. Despite this, millions of people still suffer from hunger and malnutrition, highlighting a severe imbalance in food distribution. The lack of awareness, improper storage, and inefficient redistribution systems further worsen the problem. To tackle food waste, India needs stronger policies, public awareness campaigns, and better infrastructure for food recovery and donation initiatives. By implementing sustainable food practices at the household level, a significant reduction in food wastage can be achieved.
